The Critical Problem

Railway infrastructure inspection is outdated. Manual methods are expensive, slow, and leave dangerous gaps in coverage.

🚶

Manual Inspections

Inspectors walking tracks at night, covering only small portions of the network. Human fatigue and limited visibility create dangerous blind spots.

💰

Prohibitive Costs

Specialized inspection trains cost millions and require dedicated scheduling. Most rail networks simply cannot afford comprehensive coverage.

⏰

Delayed Response

Issues are often discovered only after visual inspections or — catastrophically — after accidents occur. Reactive maintenance costs lives.

🏔️

Remote Access

Thousands of kilometers run through remote, difficult terrain. Sending teams to inspect every meter is logistically impossible.

Preventable Tragedies

History shows us that track infrastructure failures continue to claim lives. Continuous monitoring could have prevented these disasters.

January 18, 2026
🇪🇸 Adamuz, Córdoba, Spain

Adamuz High-Speed Derailment

46 deaths 292 injured

A high-speed Iryo train derailed on a straight section of the Madrid–Seville line that had been refurbished just months earlier. A second train collided with the wreckage. Track infrastructure failure is under investigation as a contributing factor.

Wikipedia → CNN Report →
How RailGuard could have helped: Continuous multi-sensor scanning would have detected track degradation, rail fractures, or ballast issues in the months between refurbishment and the accident, alerting maintenance teams before the critical failure.
October 17, 2000
🇬🇧 Hatfield, UK

Hatfield Rail Crash

4 deaths 70 injured

A high-speed train derailed when a rail fractured into over 300 pieces due to undetected fatigue cracks. The rail had been in service for decades without adequate inspection. The disaster led to the collapse of Railtrack and nationalization of UK rail infrastructure.

Wikipedia → Official Report →
How RailGuard could have helped: Visual and thermal imaging would have detected the fatigue cracks developing in the rail head months before failure, while LiDAR would have identified track geometry changes caused by deteriorating rail integrity.

The RailGuard Solution

Turn every commercial train into a high-precision inspection platform with our multi-sensor AI system.

📷

4K Visual Spectrum

High-resolution cameras capture detailed imagery of rails, fasteners, sleepers, and ballast condition at operational speeds up to 350 km/h.

🌡️

Thermal Imaging

Infrared cameras detect overheating components, friction anomalies, and stress points invisible to the naked eye.

📡

LiDAR Scanner

3D laser scanning creates millimeter-precise track geometry profiles, detecting wear, deformation, and gauge deviations.

🎯

Ground-Penetrating Radar

Subsurface imaging detects ballast degradation, voids, and drainage issues before they cause track instability.

🧠

Edge AI Processing

Onboard GPU clusters run real-time inference. Anomalies detected in milliseconds, with only alerts and metadata transmitted.
